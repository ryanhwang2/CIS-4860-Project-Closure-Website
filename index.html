üîÑ Project Retrospective

What Went Well ‚úÖ

Strong initial deliverable expectations and structures remained consistent throughout the project. Effective organization via specialized tools (Airtable, Visual Studio Code, GitHub) improved project quality and maintainability. Access to reference materials and supportive environments facilitated learning and problem-solving. Professor Espinoza's consistent feedback and my sister's time management advice were invaluable.

What Didn't Go Well ‚ö†Ô∏è

Managing time between multiple courses created significant pressure and delayed project progress. Initial scope was too broad and undefined, causing confusion about how to fulfill detailed goals. Technical issues with Git and Visual Studio Code consumed extra time deciphering vague error messages. Professional contacts were added late in the project, reducing time for relationship building.

What Would I Do Differently üîÑ

Start with well-defined, specific, and realistic scope rather than broad and general. Establish more adjustable, feasible, and segmented work schedule broken into approachable chunks. Ask for help earlier and ahead of time to avoid conflicts. Add buffer time for learning new tools and technologies. Account for external circumstances and course conflicts. Start cold outreach and networking activities much earlier in the semester.

Key Takeaways for Future Projects üéØ

  • Start with well-defined, specific, and realistic scope
  • Establish adjustable work schedule broken into simple chunks
  • Ask for help when needed and ahead of time
  • Add buffer time for learning new tools
  • Account for external circumstances and competing priorities

Story 3: Recognize and Respond to System Interactions (Part 1)

Project Presentation Story #1
üéØ Situation

Around the beginning of November 2025, I was ready to commit necessary changes to my project charter website to ensure its link had up-to-date information and I could complete that week's status report. However, Visual Studio Code wouldn't cooperate and Git now said there was an error and couldn't find the relevant repositories or branches the website was deployed on.

üìã Task

My responsibility was to resolve this issue with Git, since Git was the interface allowing my edits in Visual Studio Code to be successfully pushed and synchronized with my website and its repository of files on GitHub. The challenge was Git couldn't find the specific linked repository or branch through the Terminal commands.

‚ö° Action

I searched up this specific issue on Google and Claude.ai, including version details and minute issue details. It was discovered this was likely due to misnaming repositories (I had multiple) or misdirecting Git to wrong repository. I searched how to get Git to reference the correct repository and attempted to replicate it in Visual Studio Code. When this continued prompting errors, I used the alternative: the Version Control feature in Visual Studio Code.

‚úÖ Result

Using Version Control, I quickly discovered the new process of saving and pushing changes. After making necessary file changes, I saved them, staged the changes with descriptions, then used the "Pull, Push" option to successfully connect to the remote origin and publish the newly edited main branch. My website was updated within minutes. I learned that strong, consistent knowledge of Git, version control, and data flow is necessary to ensure project quality and timeliness.

Story 4: Recognize and Respond to System Interactions (Part 2)

Project Presentation Story #2
üéØ Situation

Deep into November, I realized my CompTIA A+ certification deliverable was becoming extremely unfeasible and unlikely to obtain. This was during CIS 4860 class, so I knew when it finished, I would have to reconsider what to do for the deliverable.

üìã Task

My responsibility was to reassess and rewrite the delivery criteria now that the initial certification deliverable was unapproachable due to large time and expenditure requirements. Given my status as a college student with numerous other courses, I couldn't afford to surrender so much. I needed to find a way to evaluate my IT knowledge in a similar but affordable and accessible manner that respected my current commitments.

‚ö° Action

I searched "Free CompTIA A+ Practice tests" on Google, Reddit, and other websites. Within search results, I read various lists of sources claiming to offer such tests, noticing their prices, features, and caveats. By repeating this process multiple times, I determined what I specifically desired and added those filters to my search. For example, I wanted one fitting current missions of actual CompTIA A+ exams, so I copied missions from official CompTIA A+ sites and pasted them onto my searches. I eventually narrowed the list to ExamCompass.com.

‚úÖ Result

Within ExamCompass.com, I completed their free exams covering CompTIA A+ components‚ÄîCore 1 and Core 2. After extensive studying, I achieved average scores exceeding 90%, well above the minimum passing score on official exams. I learned that when you have troubles with a particular element in a greater system, analyze it, its functions, qualities, and troubleshoot or find a suitable alternative. That alternative must fit much of the original's qualities or functionality to avoid compromising system functionality or quality control.

Story 5: Engage with Stakeholders

PMI Principle #3: Engage with Stakeholders
üéØ Situation

Late into November 2025, a couple weeks after my informational interviews with my Professional Network Contacts to establish a database collecting their professional information and insights with their consent, I had to commit to further communicating with them per my other deliverable, Networking Action Plan. This involved me and my four professional contacts: Rudy, Andrew, Mark, and Young.

üìã Task

My responsibility was to effectively reach out and communicate with them after the interviews to thank them for their contributions and inquire about any potential new opportunities. ‚ö° Action

I initially reached out via their provided contact methods (email, LinkedIn) to thank them. Once they replied, I took time to conceptualize what I wanted to further say. For Rudy, I emailed my current resume for review and asked for possible opportunities or job leads at Kaiser Permanente. For Andrew, I sent a LinkedIn connection request still under consideration. For Young, I sent a connection request which he accepted, and I messaged him about potential opportunities or resources to consider going forward as an IT senior.

‚úÖ Result

Young responded that he couldn't think of any at the moment but wished me good luck. Rudy responded with resume critique and general advice about referring to Kaiser Permanente Careers website. He stated if I found opportunities, he'd be happy to do an internal referral despite having never done it before. I learned that consistent, transparent, and well-meaning communication is necessary to not only introduce yourself but also build bridges for further solidarity, guidance, and mutual benefit. I gained four new professional contacts with two responding quickly, showing I have two potential new mentors to rely upon.
